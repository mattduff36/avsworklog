1. Introduction

What is the Squires App?

The Squires App is a comprehensive digital forms management system designed specifically for A&V Squires Plant Co. Ltd. It replaces paper-based processes with an easy-to-use web application that can be accessed from any device with an internet connection.

What Can You Do With This App?

  • Submit Digital Timesheets: Record your working hours each week with automatic calculations
  • Complete Vehicle Inspections: Perform daily safety checks on company vehicles
  • Access RAMS Documents: View Risk Assessments and Method Statements for your jobs
  • Request Annual Leave: Submit and track holiday requests
  • Track Vehicle Maintenance: View and update vehicle service schedules
  • Communicate with Colleagues: Send and receive messages within the system
  • Generate Reports: Create PDF and Excel reports for various purposes

Who Is This Guide For?

This guide is primarily aimed at managers and administrators, but it covers all features available to different user roles including:

  • Field Workers: Creating timesheets and inspections
  • Office Staff: Processing forms and managing data
  • Managers: Approving submissions and viewing reports
  • Administrators: Managing users and system configuration
üí° Tip: This application works best on desktop browsers (Chrome, Edge, Firefox, or Safari). Whilst it works on mobile devices, some features are optimised for larger screens.

4. Timesheets

Overview

Timesheets are used to record your working hours for each week. The system automatically calculates total hours and can detect special conditions like bank holidays and night shifts.

[Screenshot 4: Timesheets list page showing table with multiple timesheets, status badges, and filter buttons]

Viewing Your Timesheets

  1. Click the Timesheets tile on the dashboard
  2. You'll see a list of all your timesheets
  3. Each timesheet shows:
    • Week ending date
    • Total hours worked
    • Status (Draft, Pending, Approved, Rejected)
    • Who it was created for

Understanding Timesheet Status

Status What It Means What You Can Do
Draft Not yet submitted Edit and submit
Pending Awaiting manager approval View only
Approved Approved by manager View and download PDF
Rejected Needs corrections View comments, edit, and resubmit

Creating a New Timesheet

  1. Click New Timesheet button
  2. Select the week ending date (must be a Sunday)
  3. The system checks if you already have a timesheet for that week
  4. If no duplicate exists, the timesheet form will open
[Screenshot 5: Week selector page showing date picker and Continue button]

Filling In Your Timesheet

  1. Vehicle Registration: Enter the reg number of the vehicle you used
  2. For Each Day (Monday to Sunday):
    • Enter start and finish times
    • The system calculates hours automatically
    • Add job number if applicable
    • Add any remarks or notes
    • Tick special flags if needed:
      • Bank Holiday: If it was a public holiday
      • Night Shift: Automatically detected if work goes past midnight
      • Did Not Work: If you didn't work that day
[Screenshot 6: Timesheet form showing tabs for days of week, time inputs, job numbers, and checkboxes]

Special Features

Automatic Hour Calculation

When you enter start and finish times, the system automatically calculates:

  • Daily hours (including lunch break deduction if applicable)
  • Weekly total hours
  • Number of days worked

Bank Holiday Detection

The system knows about UK bank holidays and will suggest ticking the Bank Holiday box on those dates.

Night Shift Detection

If your finish time is earlier than your start time (indicating work past midnight), the Night Shift box is automatically ticked.

üí° Tip: You can still manually tick or untick the Bank Holiday and Night Shift boxes if needed.

Saving Your Work

You have two options when working on a timesheet:

  • Save Draft: Saves your progress without submitting. You can come back and finish it later
  • Submit for Approval: Completes the timesheet and sends it to your manager for review

Submitting Your Timesheet

  1. Click Submit for Approval
  2. A confirmation window appears showing a summary:
    • Total hours
    • Days worked
    • Vehicle registration
    • Day-by-day breakdown
  3. Review the summary carefully
  4. If everything is correct, click Confirm Submission
  5. If you spot an error, click Go Back to Edit
[Screenshot 7: Confirmation modal showing timesheet summary with total hours, days worked, and detailed breakdown table]
‚ö†Ô∏è Important: Once submitted, you cannot edit the timesheet unless your manager rejects it and asks for changes.

Editing a Rejected Timesheet

If your manager rejects your timesheet:

  1. Open the timesheet from your list
  2. Read the manager's comments carefully (shown at the top)
  3. Make the requested changes
  4. Click Submit for Approval again

For Managers: Approving Timesheets

If you're a manager, you can approve or reject timesheets:

  1. Go to Timesheets page
  2. Filter by "Pending" to see timesheets awaiting approval
  3. Click on a timesheet to open it
  4. Review all the details carefully
  5. Either:
    • Approve: Click the green Approve button
    • Reject: Click Reject, add comments explaining what needs to be fixed, then confirm
[Screenshot 8: Timesheet detail view showing Approve and Reject buttons, with manager view]

For Managers: Creating Timesheets for Others

Managers can create timesheets on behalf of employees:

  1. Click New Timesheet
  2. Select which employee you're creating it for from the dropdown
  3. Choose the week ending date
  4. Fill in the timesheet as normal
  5. Save as draft or submit for approval
üí° Tip: When a manager creates a timesheet for an employee, the employee can still edit it if it's in draft status.

Downloading Timesheet PDFs

Once a timesheet is approved, you can download a PDF copy:

5. Vehicle Inspections

Overview

Vehicle inspections are daily safety checks performed on company vehicles. The system uses a 26-point checklist to ensure all vehicles are safe to operate.

[Screenshot 9: Inspections list page showing table with vehicle registrations, dates, and status]

Creating a New Inspection

  1. Click Vehicle Inspections on the dashboard
  2. Click New Inspection
  3. Select the vehicle you're inspecting from the dropdown
  4. Enter the current mileage
  5. Select the week you're inspecting for

The 26-Point Inspection Checklist

For each day of the week, you'll need to check the following items:

Category Items to Check
Exterior Bodywork, mirrors, windows, lights, indicators, registration plates
Tyres Tread depth, pressure, condition, wheel nuts
Under Bonnet Oil level, coolant, washer fluid, battery
Safety Equipment First aid kit, fire extinguisher, warning triangle
Inside Cab Seat belts, horn, wipers, heater, instruments
Documents MOT, insurance, vehicle manual
[Screenshot 10: Inspection form showing tabs for days, checklist items with tick/cross/zero options]

Marking Items

For each checklist item, you have three options:

  • ‚úì (Tick): Item is satisfactory
  • ‚úó (Cross): Item has a defect that needs attention
  • 0 (Zero): Item not applicable or not checked

Recording Defects

If you mark any item with a cross (‚úó), you must provide details:

  1. When you mark an item as defective, a text box appears
  2. Describe the defect clearly (e.g., "Nearside front tyre tread below legal limit")
  3. Optionally, add a photo of the defect
  4. Indicate if the vehicle is still safe to use
üí° Tip: Clear photos of defects help the maintenance team understand and fix issues more quickly.

Adding Photos

You can attach photos to your inspection:

  1. Click the camera icon or "Add Photo" button
  2. Either:
    • Take a photo using your device's camera, or
    • Upload a photo from your device
  3. Add a description of what the photo shows
  4. The photo will be included in the inspection report

Completing the Inspection

  1. Go through each day's checklist
  2. Mark all items appropriately
  3. Add defect details and photos where needed
  4. Review your entries
  5. Click Submit Inspection
  6. The inspection is sent to your manager for review
‚ö†Ô∏è Important: If a vehicle has serious defects that make it unsafe to operate, do not use the vehicle and inform your manager immediately.

For Managers: Reviewing Inspections

Managers should regularly review vehicle inspections:

  1. Go to Vehicle Inspections
  2. Look for inspections with defects (marked with warning icon)
  3. Open the inspection to see defect details and photos
  4. Take appropriate action:
    • Schedule maintenance for minor defects
    • Take vehicle out of service for serious defects
    • Update the maintenance system with actions taken
[Screenshot 11: Inspection detail view showing defects highlighted, photos attached, and manager notes section]

6. RAMS Documents

Overview

RAMS (Risk Assessment & Method Statement) documents describe the safe way to carry out specific work tasks. All employees must read and understand the relevant RAMS before starting a job.

What is a RAMS Document?

  • Risk Assessment: Identifies hazards and risks associated with the work
  • Method Statement: Describes the safe procedure for carrying out the work
  • Control Measures: Steps to reduce or eliminate risks
  • Emergency Procedures: What to do if something goes wrong
[Screenshot 12: RAMS list page showing available documents with categories and search]

Accessing RAMS Documents

  1. Click RAMS Documents on the dashboard
  2. Browse the list of available documents
  3. Use the search box to find specific documents by name or job type
  4. Filter by category if available

Reading a RAMS Document

  1. Click on a RAMS document to open it
  2. You'll see three options:
    • Open: Read the document in your browser
    • Download: Save a PDF copy to your device
    • Email: Send a copy to your email address
  3. The system records which documents you've accessed
[Screenshot 13: RAMS document view showing Open, Download, and Email buttons]

RAMS Document Sections

A typical RAMS document includes:

  • Job Description: What work is being carried out
  • Location: Where the work will take place
  • Hazards Identified: What could cause harm
  • Persons at Risk: Who might be affected
  • Control Measures: How to stay safe
  • PPE Required: What protective equipment to wear
  • Emergency Procedures: What to do in an emergency
  • Sign-off: Who has approved this document
‚ö†Ô∏è Important: Always read the relevant RAMS document before starting a new job. If you don't understand any part of it, ask your manager for clarification before proceeding.

For Managers: Uploading RAMS Documents

Managers and admins can upload new RAMS documents:

  1. Go to RAMS Documents
  2. Click Upload New RAMS
  3. Enter document details:
    • Document name
    • Category
    • Description
    • Related job numbers (if applicable)
  4. Upload the PDF file
  5. Click Save
  6. The document is now available to all relevant staff

For Managers: Tracking Document Access

Managers can see who has accessed each RAMS document:

  • Open a RAMS document
  • Click View Access History
  • See a list showing who opened, downloaded, or emailed the document and when
  • Export this data to Excel if needed
üí° Tip: RAMS documents should be reviewed and updated regularly (at least annually) to ensure they remain accurate and relevant.

8. Vehicle Maintenance & Service

Overview

The Vehicle Maintenance & Service module helps you track and manage all vehicle maintenance activities including MOT, tax, servicing, and safety equipment checks. This replaces the old Excel spreadsheet system.

[Screenshot 17: Maintenance dashboard showing vehicle list with colour-coded status indicators for MOT, Tax, Service due dates]

What Does This Module Track?

For each vehicle, the system monitors:

  • Tax Due Date: When vehicle road tax expires
  • MOT Due Date: When the MOT test is due
  • Service Due: Mileage-based service intervals
  • Cambelt Replacement: When cambelt needs replacing
  • First Aid Kit Expiry: When first aid kit needs renewing
  • Current Mileage: Automatically updated from vehicle inspections

Understanding the Colour Coding

Colour What It Means Action Needed
Red Overdue - past the due date or mileage Urgent - schedule immediately
Amber Due Soon - within the warning threshold Plan maintenance soon
Green OK - plenty of time remaining No action needed
Grey Not Set - no due date recorded Add the date when known
üí° Tip: The warning thresholds are configurable. By default, date-based items warn 30 days in advance, and mileage-based items warn 1,000 miles in advance.

Viewing Vehicle Maintenance

  1. Click Maintenance & Service on the dashboard
  2. You'll see a list of all vehicles with their maintenance status
  3. Items needing attention are highlighted in red or amber
  4. Click on any vehicle to see full details

Alert Panels

At the top of the maintenance page, you'll see two alert panels:

  • Overdue Tasks (Red): Items that are already overdue and need immediate attention
  • Due Soon (Amber): Items that will be due within the warning threshold
[Screenshot 18: Alert panels showing overdue and due soon items with vehicle details]

Updating Maintenance Records

When maintenance is completed, update the system:

  1. Find the vehicle in the list
  2. Click Edit
  3. Update the relevant date or mileage:
    • Tax renewed? Update the Tax Due Date
    • MOT completed? Update the MOT Due Date
    • Service done? Update the Service Due Mileage
  4. Add a comment explaining what was done (minimum 10 characters required):
    • "MOT passed on [date], renewed for 12 months"
    • "Full service completed at 45,000 miles. Cost: ¬£250. Next service due at 55,000 miles"
    • "Tax renewed online, expires Aug-26"
  5. Click Save Changes
[Screenshot 19: Edit maintenance dialog showing date fields, mileage inputs, and mandatory comment box]
‚ö†Ô∏è Important: You must add a comment every time you update a maintenance record. This creates an audit trail showing what was done and when.

Automatic Mileage Updates

The system automatically updates vehicle mileage:

  • When a vehicle inspection is submitted, the current mileage is recorded
  • This mileage is automatically copied to the maintenance system
  • You don't need to manually enter mileage - it happens automatically
  • The "Last Updated" timestamp shows when the mileage was last refreshed

Viewing Maintenance History

To see all changes made to a vehicle's maintenance records:

  1. Click History button next to the vehicle
  2. You'll see a chronological list of all updates showing:
    • What changed (old value ‚Üí new value)
    • Who made the change
    • When it was changed
    • The comment explaining why
[Screenshot 20: Maintenance history dialog showing timeline of changes with dates, users, and comments]

For Admins: Configuring Maintenance Categories

Administrators and managers can customise the maintenance system:

  1. Go to Maintenance & Service
  2. Click the Settings tab (gear icon)
  3. Here you can:
    • Add new maintenance categories (e.g., "Tyre Replacement")
    • Edit existing categories
    • Change warning thresholds (e.g., warn 60 days before MOT instead of 30)
    • Delete unused categories
üí° Tip: Changes to warning thresholds take effect immediately and apply to all vehicles.

Sorting and Searching

To find specific vehicles or sort the list:

  • Search: Type a registration number in the search box
  • Sort: Click any column header to sort by that column
  • Filter: Click the overdue or due soon panels to filter the list

12. Common Scenarios

Here are some typical situations and how to handle them using the system:

Scenario 1: Starting a New Week

Situation: It's Monday morning and you're starting a new working week.

What to do:

  1. Log in to the application
  2. Go to Vehicle Inspections
  3. Create a new inspection for your vehicle for this week
  4. Complete Monday's inspection checklist before starting work
  5. If any defects are found, photograph them and report immediately

Scenario 2: Submitting Your Weekly Timesheet

Situation: It's Sunday evening and you need to submit your timesheet for the week just ended.

What to do:

  1. Log in to the application
  2. Click Timesheets ‚Üí New Timesheet
  3. Select the Sunday just passed as the week ending date
  4. Fill in your hours for each day, along with job numbers
  5. Add vehicle registration
  6. Review the summary carefully
  7. Click Submit for Approval
  8. Check the confirmation modal and submit
  9. Your manager will be notified and will approve it

Scenario 3: Booking a Week's Holiday

13. Troubleshooting

Common Issues and Solutions

I can't log in - "Invalid email or password" error

Solution:

  • Check you're using the correct email address (the one provided by your administrator)
  • Check caps lock isn't on when typing your password
  • Try clicking "Forgot Password?" to reset your password
  • If still having issues, contact your administrator

I can't see certain features that others can

Solution:

  • Your user role might not have permissions for those features
  • Check with your manager or administrator about your role
  • They can update your permissions if needed

The page won't load or keeps spinning

Solution:

  • Check your internet connection is working
  • Try refreshing the page (F5 or Ctrl+R)
  • Clear your browser cache and cookies
  • Try a different browser
  • If the problem persists, it might be a system issue - contact your administrator

My timesheet won't submit - validation errors

Solution:

  • Read the error messages carefully - they tell you what's wrong
  • Common issues:
    • Missing vehicle registration
    • Times not entered for all days
    • Finish time before start time (unless night shift)
    • Missing job numbers
  • Correct the highlighted issues and try submitting again

I accidentally submitted the wrong timesheet

Solution:

  • If it's still in "Pending" status, you cannot edit it
  • Contact your manager immediately and ask them to reject it
  • Explain what needs changing
  • Once rejected, you can edit and resubmit

The vehicle I need isn't in the dropdown list

Solution:

  • The vehicle might not have been added to the system yet
  • Contact your administrator and ask them to add it
  • They need to go to Admin ‚Üí Vehicles ‚Üí Add Vehicle

I can't remember which vehicle I used last week

Solution:

  • Go to Vehicle Inspections and look at your previous inspections
  • The vehicle registration is shown on each inspection
  • Or check your previous timesheets

My leave request was rejected but I don't know why

Solution:

  • Open the rejected leave request
  • Your manager should have added comments explaining the reason
  • If there are no comments, speak to your manager directly
  • Once you understand the issue, you can submit a new request for different dates

I need to access the system on my phone but it's not working well

Solution:

  • The system is designed primarily for desktop/laptop browsers
  • It does work on mobile, but some features are better on a larger screen
  • For best results:
    • Use landscape orientation on your phone
    • Use Chrome browser on Android or Safari on iPhone
    • For complex tasks like filling timesheets, try to use a computer if possible

Photos aren't uploading to my vehicle inspection

Solution:

  • Check the photo file size - very large photos might fail
  • Try taking a new photo at a lower resolution
  • Make sure your internet connection is stable
  • Ensure you've given the browser permission to access your camera

I'm getting a message saying the system can't find my profile

Solution:

  • This usually means your account isn't fully set up
  • Contact your administrator immediately
  • They may need to recreate your account properly

Getting Help

If you can't resolve an issue using this guide:

  1. First: Try the solutions above for your specific issue
  2. Then: Use the internal messaging system to ask a colleague or your manager
  3. If still stuck: Contact your system administrator with:
    • A description of the problem
    • What you were trying to do
    • Any error messages you saw
    • Screenshots if possible
